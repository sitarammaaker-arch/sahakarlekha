# Chapter 28 — Full Case Study: Rania Cooperative Society (a full year)

> **Goal:** the accounting of a whole year — from opening balances to final accounts and appropriation — in one thread that ties out at ₹6,79,000.

## 28.1 Introduction
Rania Multipurpose Cooperative Society; FY 2026-27; trade in fertiliser and consumer goods; 100 opening members.

## 28.2 Opening balances (1 April 2026)

| Assets | ₹ | Liabilities & Capital | ₹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cash | 20,000 | Share capital | 2,00,000 |
| Bank | 1,50,000 | Statutory reserve | 50,000 |
| Stock | 0 | Creditors | 40,000 |
| Godown (net) | 3,00,000 | Bank loan | 2,00,000 |
| Debtors | 92,500 | Accumulated surplus | 72,500 |
| **Total** | **5,62,500** | **Total** | **5,62,500** |

(*Note: opening Dr = Cr = ₹5,62,500; the basis of the year’s transactions.*)

## 28.3 Main transactions of the year
1. 20 new members, 10 shares @ ₹100 + ₹50 admission fee → share capital ₹20,000, admission fee ₹1,000 (cash ₹21,000).
2. Purchase: 90 bags fertiliser @ ₹2,200 = ₹1,98,000 (on credit, IFFCO).
3. Sale: 90 bags @ ₹2,250 = ₹2,02,500 (cash).
4. Salary ₹30,000 (with ₹2,400 EPF deduction; net ₹27,600 cash).
5. Rent ₹12,000 cash.
6. Interest income ₹5,000 in bank.
7. Depreciation godown 10% SLM = ₹30,000 (godown net ₹2,70,000).
8. Debtor collection ₹92,500; creditor payment ₹40,000.
9. EPF payable ₹2,400 deposited.

## 28.7 Balance Sheet (31 March 2027)

| Liabilities & Capital | ₹ | Assets | ₹ |

| Statutory reserve | 50,000 | Stock (closing) | 0 |
| Accumulated surplus | 11,000 | Debtors (92,500−92,500) | 0 |
| Creditors (40,000+1,98,000−40,000) | 1,98,000 | Godown (net) | 2,70,000 |
| Bank loan | 2,00,000 | | |
| **Total** | **6,79,000** | **Total** | **6,79,000** |

(*Both sides meet at ₹6,79,000 — the whole year ties. From the opening ₹5,62,500, after new capital ₹21,000 and the year’s transactions, this is the final balance.*)

> 💡 **Lesson:** the opening was balanced, every entry was Dr = Cr, and the closing stock used the one formula — so the Balance Sheet tallied on its own. This is the essence of the whole book.

## 28.8 Profit Distribution
There is a net deficit this year, so no appropriation; the deficit is adjusted against accumulated surplus (₹72,500 → ₹11,000). *If there were a surplus, first ≥25% to the statutory reserve, then dividend/patronage.*

## 28.9 Takeaways
- The opening Dr = Cr is the foundation of the whole year’s tally.
- Heavy depreciation/salary can produce a deficit — yet the books still balance.
- Closing stock 0 was correct (purchases = sales).
- Every report is logically connected to the others.
