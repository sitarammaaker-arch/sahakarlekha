# Chapter 3 — Chart of Accounts

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** tell a group from a sub-ledger; understand the code system; the standard chart; add a new account.

## 3.1 What is the chart of accounts
The "map" of accounts — which account belongs to which category. A new society comes with a ready cooperative standard chart (Ledger Heads).

## 3.2 Group vs sub-ledger
A group = a heading only; a sub-ledger (Ledger) = where the actual entry goes. Example: Share Capital (1100, group) → Individual Share Capital (1102, sub-ledger).

> 💡 Entries always go in a sub-ledger, never in a group.

## 3.3 The code system

| First digit | Category | In report |
|---|---|---|
| 1000 | Capital & funds | Balance Sheet (liability) |
| 2000 | Liabilities | Balance Sheet (liability) |
| 3000 | Assets | Balance Sheet (asset) |
| 4000 | Income | Income & Expenditure / Trading |
| 5000 | Expenses | Income & Expenditure / Trading |

## 3.5 Cooperative-specific accounts

| Account | Why special |
|---|---|
| Statutory Reserve (1201) | At least 25% of profit |
| Patronage Rebate (4406) | Return on members’ transactions (appropriation) |
| Share-capital sub-types | Government / individual / institutional separately |

> ⚠️ Do not put patronage/dividend in expenses — they are appropriations.

## 3.6 Adding a new account
Ledger Heads → Add Account → name, parent group, opening balance → Save. When you add a supplier/customer the app auto-creates the account in the correct group.

## 3.7 System accounts
System accounts like Cash and Share Capital cannot be deleted. Don’t delete an account that has entries — rename it instead (so the audit trail stays intact).

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Entry directly in a group | Always in a sub-ledger |
| Sales under 5000 | 4000 = income |
| All purchases in one account | Activity accounts (5110…) |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Account classification follows the standard chart; funds are shown separately."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
At least 25% of net profit must go to the Statutory Reserve (1201).

## ✅ Worked example
Correct category: godown = asset; GST payable = liability; fertiliser sales = income (4101); salary = expense; share capital = capital (1102).

## 📚 Case study
All sales in one "Sales" account → no item-wise profit visible. After adopting activity accounts, the Trading Account showed each item’s profit separately.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Must I create every account?** No — a ready chart comes; suppliers/customers attach automatically.
**Q. Can I delete the Cash account?** No, it is a system account.
