# Chapter 16 — Balance Sheet

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** assets = liabilities + capital; the single closing-stock figure; avoiding double-counting; reading it.

## 16.1 What it is
The society’s position on a date: assets on one side, liabilities + capital on the other; the two equal.

## 16.2 Format

| Liabilities & Capital | ₹ | Assets | ₹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Share capital | 2,00,000 | Cash & bank | 61,000 |
| Reserve fund | 50,000 | Stock (closing) | 70,000 |
| Net surplus | 41,000 | Debtors | 20,000 |
| Creditors | 40,000 | Godown (net) | 3,00,000 |
| Bank loan | 2,00,000 | | |
| **Total** | **5,31,000** | **Total** | **4,51,000** |

(*This is a format illustration; the real numbers appear in the case study, Chapter 28.*)

## 16.3 Closing stock — a single figure
The Balance Sheet’s stock = the Trading Account’s closing stock = the canonical value. The same in all three places.

> ⚠️ Audit fix: when closing stock wasn’t journalled, the Balance Sheet double-counted with the opening stock — now fixed. The closing journal goes to a dedicated account (5150).

## 16.4 Fixed assets at net value
Godown/equipment = original cost − accumulated depreciation (WDV).

## 16.5 Unposted closing-stock account
If the 3400-group isn’t posted yet, it’s excluded from assetLeaves — preventing double-counting.

## 16.6 Reading it
Capital + reserves = the strength of member funds; high debtors/stock = focus on collection/sales; cash = liquidity.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Counting stock twice | The one formula |
| Fixed assets at original cost | At WDV |
| Not adding the surplus | On the capital side |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Balance Sheet balanced; closing stock a single figure; fixed assets at WDV."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
A cooperative Balance Sheet is fund-wise; member funds and external liabilities shown separately.

## ✅ Worked example (mini)
Assets: cash 10,000; stock 15,000; debtors 5,000 = 30,000. Liabilities+capital: share capital 20,000; creditors 5,000; surplus 5,000 = 30,000. Balanced.

## 📚 Case study
With closing stock not journalled, the Balance Sheet showed as much extra as the opening stock. Journalling to the dedicated account fixed it.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. If it won’t tally?** Check surplus/opening/stock.
**Q. When to prepare it?** Year-end; any time you like.
