# Chapter 29 — Special Registers & Records

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** the member, share, loan, stock, salary, GST/TDS registers; their purpose and reconciliation.

## 29.1 Why
A register = a statutory record; the basis of audit and queries. The app builds most of them automatically.

## 29.2 Key registers

| Register | What | In the app |
|---|---|---|
| Member | Name, date, shares | Members |
| Share | Share count and capital | Members/1102 |
| Loan | Loan, recovery, outstanding | Loans |
| Stock | Inward-outward, balance | Inventory |
| Salary | Slip, deductions, net | Salary |
| GST | Output/input/HSN | GST Summary |
| TDS | Deduction, 26Q | TDS Register |
| Sale/Purchase | Bill, status | Registers |

## 29.3 Reconciliation
Each register must agree with the relevant ledger/account (share register = 1102; TDS-payable register = 2202).

## 29.4 Export
Each register as PDF/Excel — into the audit file and the backup.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Not reconciling a register | Reconcile to the account |
| Manual copying | Export from the app |
| Not keeping it current | Regular entry |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Statutory registers up to date and reconciled with the relevant accounts."

## 📘 Standard
Registers are mandatory per the Cooperative Act/bye-laws.

## ✅ Worked example
Share register total ₹2,20,000 = balance of account 1102 = Balance Sheet — all three match.

## 📚 Case study
A difference was found between the TDS-payable register and account 2202 — a deposit entry was missed; correcting it reconciled them.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Build them all by hand?** No — the app builds them; you reconcile and export.
**Q. Which is most important?** Member and share (the foundation of a cooperative).
