# Chapter 6 — The Voucher System: Receipt, Payment, Journal & Contra

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** the four voucher types; Easy and Expert modes; approval and cancelling vouchers correctly.

## 6.1 The four types

| Voucher | When | Tell-tale |
|---|---|---|
| Receipt | Money came in | "We received" |
| Payment | Money went out | "We paid" |
| Journal | Adjustment without cash/bank | "Only between accounts" |
| Contra | Cash ↔ bank | "Moved place" |

## 6.2 Which one when
Share capital from a member = receipt; salary = payment; depreciation = journal; cash deposited in bank = contra.

## 6.3 Easy mode
Vouchers → Easy → pick a template ("Member share capital", "Salary", "Rent") → date, amount, note → Save. Accounts are pre-set.

## 6.4 Expert mode
Multi-line: each account, Dr/Cr, amount. Save only when "Balanced ✓" shows below.

> ⚠️ If total Dr ≠ total Cr, the app won’t let you save.

## 6.5 Contra (cash ↔ bank)
₹50,000 cash deposited in bank:

| Account | Dr | Cr |
|---|---|---|
| Bank | 50,000 | |
| Cash | | 50,000 |

> 💡 Don’t make a contra a receipt/payment — that double-counts the Receipts & Payments account.

## 6.6 Voucher number, member-link, note
Number is automatic; a member-link puts the entry in the member ledger; write a clear note.

## 6.7 Approval
"Send for approval" → in Voucher Approval an Admin accepts/rejects.

## 6.8 Cancel ≠ delete
Don’t erase an entry — cancel it (with a reason). A cancelled voucher stays in the list but is out of every report; its number is preserved.

> ⚠️ Sale/purchase vouchers are not cancelled from Vouchers — remove them from Sale/Purchase Management (cascade).

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Contra as receipt/payment | Cash↔bank = contra |
| Vague note | A clear narration |
| Deleting a voucher | Cancel it |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Vouchers are sequential; cancelled vouchers carry a reason and are excluded from results."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
Every voucher carries date, number, accounts, amount, narration, and an authorising signature.

## ✅ Worked examples
Receipt: Dr Bank 2,000 / Cr Interest Income 2,000. Payment: Dr Rent 5,000 / Cr Cash 5,000. Journal: Dr Depreciation 10,000 / Cr Accumulated Depreciation 10,000. Contra: Dr Bank 50,000 / Cr Cash 50,000.

## 📚 Case study
Every bank deposit was made a "receipt" → income overstated. Fix: cash↔bank is always a contra.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Easy or Expert?** Day-to-day = Easy; complex = Expert.
**Q. A wrong voucher?** Cancel it, then make the correct one.
