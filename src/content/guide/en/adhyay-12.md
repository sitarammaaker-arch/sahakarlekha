# Chapter 12 — Day Book & Ledger

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** read the Day Book; an account’s ledger and running balance; search/filter; the audit trail.

## 12.1 What is the Day Book
A date-ordered list of every voucher — the society’s "diary". Date, voucher no., type, accounts, amount.

## 12.2 Opening & filtering
Reports → Day Book → filter by date range/type/account → the list. Click a row → the full voucher.

## 12.3 The Ledger
Every transaction of an account on one page: inward on the Dr side, outward on the Cr side, with a running balance.

| Date | Particulars | Dr | Cr | Balance |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 01/04 | Opening | | | 10,000 |
| 05/04 | Sale collection | 6,000 | | 16,000 |
| 10/04 | Rent | | 5,000 | 11,000 |

## 12.4 What the running balance means
Cash/asset → Dr balance; liability/income → Cr balance. The moment-to-moment position.

## 12.5 Member/party ledger
Member ledger = share capital and transactions; customer/supplier ledger = dues.

## 12.6 Audit trail
Who created/changed each voucher and when (editHistory) — cancellations carry a reason too.

## 12.7 Search
Search by amount/particulars/party — "how much to IFFCO this year?" in seconds.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Treating the Day Book as a report | It is a list |
| Wrong date range | Check the range |
| Not reconciling the ledger | Review periodically |

## 🔍 Audit note
"The Day Book is sequential; every entry is reflected in the ledger; the audit trail is available."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
The Day Book and Ledger are primary records — keeping them safe is mandatory.

## ✅ Worked example
Cash ledger: opening 5,000; sale 2,000(Dr); rent 1,500(Cr); balance = 5,500.

## 📚 Case study
The auditor asked for "total paid to IFFCO". A ledger search found it instantly — it used to take hours.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Day Book vs Ledger?** Day Book is time-ordered, Ledger is account-wise.
**Q. Can I edit the ledger?** Not directly — fix the voucher and the ledger updates automatically.
