# Chapter 4 — Opening Balances

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** carry last year’s closing into this year’s opening; tally Dr = Cr; understand the root of "the Balance Sheet won’t tally".

## 4.1 What is an opening balance
For an already-running society, the 31 March closing balance = the 1 April opening balance of the next year. One year’s closing = the next year’s opening.

## 4.2 The golden rule: opening too must be Dr = Cr
The total of all accounts’ opening balances must have total Dr = total Cr.

> ⚠️ This is the number-one reason a Balance Sheet won’t tally.

## 4.3 What to bring

| Category | Account | Side |
|---|---|---|
| Assets | Cash, bank, stock, debtors, fixed assets (net) | Dr |
| Liabilities | Creditors, loans, deposits | Cr |
| Capital | Share capital, funds, accumulated surplus | Cr |

Source: last year’s audited Balance Sheet.

## 4.4 Filling it in the app
Society Setup → Opening Balances → for each account the amount + Dr/Cr → fill until total Dr = total Cr → Save.

## 4.5 Stock opening (a double entry)

| Where | What |
|---|---|
| Inventory → item | opening qty and rate |
| Stock account (3400) | total value (Dr) |

> ⚠️ Fill both — otherwise quantity/value mismatch.

## 4.6 Debtor/creditor opening
Party-wise opening (debtors Dr, creditors Cr) — so bill-wise collection/payment runs correctly.

## 4.7 Checking
Open the Trial Balance — if total Dr = total Cr, it’s right. If there’s a difference, the app’s diagnostic says "opening gap ₹X".

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Total Dr ≠ total Cr | Fill until equal |
| Stock in one place | item + account both |
| Skipping surplus (1208) | Put the remaining difference there |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Opening balances verified by reconciliation with the previous year’s audited Balance Sheet."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
Accumulated funds must be carried forward as-is — not started from zero.

## ✅ Worked example
Previous closing: assets (cash 10,000; bank 50,000; stock 1,87,000; debtors 20,000; godown 3,00,000) = 5,67,000; liabilities+capital (share capital 2,00,000; reserve 50,000; creditors 40,000; loan 2,00,000; surplus 77,000) = 5,67,000. Fill in the app with Dr/Cr → the Trial Balance meets at 5,67,000 on both sides.

## 📚 Case study
Forgot to enter surplus (1208) → Balance Sheet off by ₹77,000. Putting it on Cr fixed it. **Lesson:** the remaining difference is usually accumulated surplus.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. A brand-new society?** Opening is zero; enter directly.
**Q. Stock opening rate?** At last year’s cost rate.
