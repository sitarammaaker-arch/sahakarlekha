# Chapter 34 — Financial Indicators, Ratios & Society Lifecycle

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** measure society health — recovery %, current ratio, margin, NPA; and record entries from formation to dissolution.

## 34.1 Key indicators
| Indicator | Formula | Good |
|---|---|---|
| Recovery % | recovered ÷ total due × 100 | 90%+ |
| Current ratio | current assets ÷ current liabilities | 1.5–2 |
| Gross profit rate | gross profit ÷ sales × 100 | watch the trend |
| Interest spread | loan rate − deposit rate | positive |
| NPA % | non-performing loans ÷ total loans | as low as possible |
| Stock turnover | cost of goods ÷ average stock | high |

## 34.2 Using the indicators
Compute them each year-end, compare with last year, and tell the board in plain language. Class 'A' = good recovery + low NPA + timeliness.

## 34.3 Formation
Founder shares: Dr Cash / Cr Share Capital. Formation expense: Dr Preliminary Expense / Cr Cash.

## 34.4 Operation & growth
The daily cycle (chapters 6–17). Expansion: godown (on loan) Dr Building / Cr Bank Loan.

## 34.5 Trouble period
Loss → to accumulated loss. Doubtful loans → NPA provision: Dr Provision Expense / Cr Bad-Loan Provision.

## 34.6 Dissolution
Sell assets → first pay **external liabilities** → finally return member capital. A liquidator/departmental guidance is mandatory.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes
| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Dividend in a loss | Only out of profit |
| Unrecovered interest as income | Interest Suspense |
| Capital before liabilities in dissolution | Liabilities first |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Recovery trend; NPA classification and provision; adequate spread; compare indicators with last year."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
Indicators link to the audit class; transparent reporting at the general body.

## ✅ Worked examples
Due ₹10,00,000, recovered ₹8,50,000 → recovery 85%. Current assets ₹4,00,000 ÷ liabilities ₹2,00,000 = 2.0.

## 📚 Case study
Credit society: loan interest 12% − deposit interest 8% = spread 4% → the main earning; if recovery falls, even the spread is useless.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Profit but no cash?** Profit is stuck in receivables/stock; check the R&P.
**Q. NPA rising?** Increase provision, plan recovery.
