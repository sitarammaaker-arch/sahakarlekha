# Chapter 5 — Members & Membership Management

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** add and approve members; record share capital and admission fee separately; the member ledger; share refund on exit.

## 5.1 Members are the pivot
"One member, one vote." The main source of capital is members’ share capital; profit returns to them as dividend and patronage rebate.

## 5.2 Types of membership
Regular (share capital, full rights) and nominal (limited rights).

## 5.3 Adding a new member
Members → Add Member → name, join date, number of shares and share capital → Save (member ID and account created automatically).

> 💡 Enter the join date correctly — it is the basis of the Federation report.

## 5.4 Application & approval
Member Application → pending applications → Approve/Reject. Only approved members are counted in reports/dividend.

## 5.5 Recording share capital
Ram bought 10 shares @ ₹100 = ₹1,000 in cash:

| Account | Dr | Cr |
|---|---|---|
| Cash | 1,000 | |
| Individual Share Capital (1102) | | 1,000 |

> ⚠️ Do not treat share capital as **income** — it is capital.

## 5.6 Admission fee (this is income)
₹1,000 share capital + ₹50 admission fee, total ₹1,050:

| Account | Dr | Cr |
|---|---|---|
| Cash | 1,050 | |
| Share Capital (1102) | | 1,000 |
| Admission Fee (income) | | 50 |

## 5.7 The member ledger
Each member has a separate ledger. The sum of all members’ share capital = balance of account 1102 = Balance Sheet/return.

## 5.8 Share refund on exit
Dr Share Capital (1102) / Cr Cash-bank. Make the member **inactive**, do not delete.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Share capital as income | 1102 (capital) |
| Admission fee as capital | Income |
| Counting before approval | Approve first |
| Deleting a member | Make inactive |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Member register up to date; share capital total reconciled with 1102; admission fee classified as income."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
At least one share is mandatory per member; share capital = owner’s capital, not a loan.

## ✅ Worked example
Three new members, each 10 shares @ ₹100 + ₹50 admission fee in cash:

| Account | Dr | Cr |
|---|---|---|
| Cash | 3,150 | |
| Share Capital (1102) | | 3,000 |
| Admission Fee (income) | | 150 |

## 📚 Case study
Kept adding admission fee into share capital → capital overstated, income understated. After the fix, admission fee sits separately in income.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. A member buys more shares?** A fresh entry (Dr cash/bank, Cr 1102).
**Q. Where is total share capital?** Members page = balance of account 1102.
