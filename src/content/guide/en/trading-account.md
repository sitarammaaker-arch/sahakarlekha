# Chapter 14 — Trading Account

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** gross profit; opening + purchases − closing; activity-wise breakdown; the correct closing-stock value.

## 14.1 What is the Trading Account
The result of buying and selling = **gross profit/loss**. Formula: gross profit = sales − cost of goods sold; cost of goods sold = opening stock + purchases − closing stock.

## 14.2 Format

| Expenditure side | ₹ | Income side | ₹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening stock | 20,000 | Sales | 90,000 |
| Purchases | 55,000 | Closing stock | 70,000 |
| Gross profit (c/d) | 85,000 | | |
| **Total** | **1,60,000** | **Total** | **1,60,000** |

## 14.3 Activity-wise
With per-item accounts (4101/5110…) the Trading Account shows each category’s gross profit (fertiliser, consumer goods…) separately.

## 14.4 The correct closing-stock value
Closing stock = canonical qty × weighted-average cost (the one formula). This is how the Trading Account and Balance Sheet match.

## 14.5 In the app
Reports → Trading Account → built automatically; PDF export.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Closing stock at sale price | At cost |
| Skipping direct expenses | Add freight etc. |
| Treating gross as net | I&E remains |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Gross profit is activity-wise; closing stock at cost; quantity-value reconciled."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
The gross result of trading shown separately; closing stock per AS-2.

## ✅ Worked example
Opening 0; purchase 90 bags ₹2,200 = 1,98,000; sale 90 bags ₹2,250 = 2,02,500; closing 0 → gross profit = 2,02,500 − 1,98,000 = ₹4,500.

## 📚 Case study
Closing stock showed ₹0 (from purchase-rate 0). After valuing at weighted-average cost, the gross profit was correct.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. A service-only society?** Trading Account minimal; straight to I&E.
**Q. A gross loss?** Purchase/cost > sales.
