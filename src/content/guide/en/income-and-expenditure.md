# Chapter 15 — Income & Expenditure Account

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** net surplus/deficit; the transfer of gross profit; direct vs indirect expenses; capital vs revenue.

## 15.1 What it is
A cooperative’s "profit & loss account" = Income & Expenditure. Net surplus = (gross profit + other income) − (operating expenses).

## 15.2 Format

| Expenditure | ₹ | Income | ₹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Salary | 30,000 | Gross profit (b/d) | 85,000 |
| Rent | 12,000 | Interest income | 5,000 |
| Depreciation | 8,000 | Admission fee | 1,000 |
| Net surplus | 41,000 | | |
| **Total** | **91,000** | **Total** | **91,000** |

## 15.3 The transfer of gross profit
The Trading Account’s gross profit flows automatically into the income side of I&E (audit fix C-9).

## 15.4 Capital vs revenue
Only revenue items here; capital ones (godown purchase, loan repayment) are not here — in the Balance Sheet/cash flow.

> ⚠️ Don’t put a godown purchase in expenses — it’s an asset.

## 15.5 Direct/indirect expenses
Direct (freight) → Trading; indirect (salary, rent, depreciation) → I&E.

## 15.6 In the app
Reports → Income & Expenditure → automatic; the net surplus goes on to Profit Distribution.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Capital as revenue | Into asset/liability |
| Skipping gross profit | It now flows in automatically |
| Forgetting depreciation | Journal it |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Income/expenditure classification appropriate; capital-revenue separation correct; net surplus verified."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
In a cooperative, "profit" is called the **surplus**; the amount before appropriation.

## ✅ Worked example
Gross profit 4,500; interest income 500; salary 2,000; rent 1,000 → net surplus = (4,500+500) − 3,000 = ₹2,000.

## 📚 Case study
A godown repair (capital improvement) was put in revenue expense → surplus understated, asset understated. Correct classification fixed it.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Surplus and profit the same?** Yes, cooperative terminology.
**Q. Where does a deficit go?** Reduces the accumulated surplus.
