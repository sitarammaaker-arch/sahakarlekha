# Chapter 17 — Receipts & Payments Account

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** a cash-basis summary; capital vs revenue; excluding contra; opening/closing cash & bank.

## 17.1 What it is
All cash/bank receipts and payments through the year (cash basis) — a mandatory cooperative statement.

## 17.2 Format

| Receipts | ₹ | Payments | ₹ |
|---|---|---|---|
| Opening cash/bank | 60,000 | Purchases | 1,98,000 |
| Sale collections | 2,02,500 | Salary | 30,000 |
| Share capital | 20,000 | Rent | 12,000 |
| | | Closing cash/bank | 42,500 |
| **Total** | **2,82,500** | **Total** | **2,82,500** |

## 17.3 Capital vs revenue
**Both** appear here (cash basis) — share capital, loans too; but keep the classification right (audit fix C-11/12).

## 17.4 Excluding contra
A cash↔bank transfer must not be counted on both sides — else it doubles. The app counts each contra/non-cash counterparty only once.

> ⚠️ Earlier multiple cash/bank lines were double-counted — now each counterparty once.

## 17.5 Opening/closing balance — the sign
Opening cash/bank is taken signed (Dr/Cr); closing = opening + receipts − payments.

## 17.6 Difference from I&E

| Income & Expenditure | Receipts & Payments |
|---|---|
| Accrual basis | Cash basis |
| Revenue only | Capital + revenue |
| Tells the surplus | Tells the cash flow |

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Contra twice | Count it once |
| Revenue only | Add capital too |
| Opening sign reversed | Signed opening |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Receipts & Payments reconciled with the cash book; transfer entries excluded; closing balance verified."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
The Receipts & Payments account is statutory; presented at the AGM and audit.

## ✅ Worked example
Opening 60,000; receipts 2,22,500; payments 2,40,000 → closing = 60,000 + 2,22,500 − 2,40,000 = ₹42,500.

## 📚 Case study
A bank-deposit contra was counted on both sides → totals ballooned. After counting once, it reconciled with the cash book.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Is this the same as I&E?** No — cash vs accrual.
**Q. Where does a loan repayment go?** Here on the payment side; not in I&E.
