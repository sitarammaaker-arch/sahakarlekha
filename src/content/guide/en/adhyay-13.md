# Chapter 13 — Trial Balance

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** build and read a trial balance; why Dr = Cr matters; the NCDC two-section format; diagnosing a mismatch.

## 13.1 What is a trial balance
A list of all accounts’ balances on a date; total Dr = total Cr → proof of arithmetical accuracy.

## 13.2 Building it
Reports → Trial Balance → date → it’s built. Each account’s balance on its correct side; both totals equal below.

## 13.3 The NCDC two-section format
A cooperative standard: above, capital/liability/asset (Balance Sheet items); below, income/expense (result items) — two sections. The app gives exactly this format.

## 13.4 Example

## 13.5 When it won’t tally
App diagnostic: "opening gap ₹X" or an unbalanced voucher. Usual causes: opening Dr ≠ Cr; an unbalanced manual journal.

> 💡 The app saves a voucher only when Dr = Cr, so imbalance usually comes from the opening.

## 13.6 Beyond the trial balance
Trial Balance → Trading Account → Income & Expenditure → Balance Sheet. All are built from it.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Treating Dr=Cr as proof of correct posting | It is only arithmetical |
| Wrong date | The correct FY |
| Ignoring the opening | Tally it first |

## 🔍 Audit note
"The trial balance is balanced; balances reconcile with the ledgers; the two-section format is standard-compliant."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
The trial balance in NCDC/cooperative format; the starting point of the audit.

## ✅ Worked example
Cash 5,000(Dr); Share Capital 5,000(Cr); Purchases 10,000(Dr); Creditors 10,000(Cr); Sales 2,000(Cr); cash-sale 2,000(Dr) → total Dr 17,000 = total Cr 17,000.

## 📚 Case study
The trial balance was off by ₹77,000 — the accumulated-surplus opening was missing. Adding it tallied it.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. If it tallies, is everything correct?** Arithmetic is correct; a right amount in a wrong account can still hide.
**Q. How often?** Month-end and before audit.
