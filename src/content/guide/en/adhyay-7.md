# Chapter 7 — Sales Management

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** record cash/bank/credit sales; handle GST; understand stock linkage and the oversell block; read the Sale Register.

## 7.1 Three effects

A sale: increases income, reduces stock, and brings in cash/receivables.

## 7.2 Cash / credit sale
Cash: Dr Cash 5,000 / Cr Fertiliser Sales (4101) 5,000.
Credit: Dr Customer (3303) 5,000 / Cr Fertiliser Sales 5,000.

## 7.3 GST sale
₹10,000 consumer goods, 18% (intra-state), cash:

| Account | Dr | Cr |
|---|---|---|
| Cash | 11,800 | |
| Consumer Sales (4103) | | 10,000 |
| CGST Payable | | 900 |
| SGST Payable | | 900 |

> ⚠️ GST is not the society’s income — it is payable to the government.

## 7.4 In the app
Sales → date, customer, items (qty, rate), payment mode → Save (stock drops, bill number generated).

## 7.5 Stock linkage & the oversell block
A sale of more than the available quantity (or of a 0-stock item) is **blocked** — the app shows a red warning.

| Qty ≤ available | Will save |
|---|---|
| Qty > available | Blocked — purchase first |

## 7.6 Per-item sales account
Each item posts to its own activity account (4101/4103…) → the Trading Account shows item-wise profit.

## 7.7 Sale Register
Date-wise sales, GST and status: outstanding/partial/settled. PDF/Excel export.

## 7.8 Edit / delete
Only from Sale Management — stock returns, voucher cancelled, customer ledger corrected (all cascade).

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| GST counted as income | Keep it separate |
| Not choosing a customer on credit | Always select one |
| Everything in one account | Activity accounts |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Cash and credit sales are separated; GST liability is separate; stock issues reconcile with sales."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
Sales are mostly member service; a record of patronage is important.

## ✅ Worked examples
Cash: Dr Cash 800 / Cr Seed Sales 800. Credit: Dr Shyam 3,000 / Cr Fertiliser Sales 3,000. GST: Dr Bank 2,360 / Cr Consumer Sales 2,000 / Cr CGST 180 / Cr SGST 180.

## 📚 Case study
85 bags were bought, but a stale-stock bug let 90 be sold. Fix: the oversell block — now you cannot sell more than is available.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. A cash (walk-in) customer?** Yes, just write the name; a registered customer is required for credit.
**Q. No stock but the customer wants it?** Record the purchase first.
