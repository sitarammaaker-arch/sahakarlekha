# Chapter 10 — Bill-wise Settlement

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** apply a payment against a specific bill; partial and oldest-first; advance/on-account; ageing of dues.

## 10.1 The problem
"Which bill is this money for?" — bill-wise settlement answers exactly that.

## 10.2 Four methods

| Method | When |
|---|---|
| New Reference | A credit sale/purchase — a new bill |
| Against Reference | Payment against an existing bill |
| Advance | Before the bill |
| On Account | Bill uncertain |

## 10.3 How a credit bill is created
Credit sale: Dr Ram (3303) 10,000 / Cr Sales 10,000 → ₹10,000 due from Ram (INV-12).

## 10.4 Collecting from a customer (Receive Payment)
Receive Payment → customer → outstanding bills → amount against each bill (partial too) → cash/bank → record. Or "allocate to oldest bills first".
Ram pays ₹6,000: Dr Cash 6,000 / Cr Ram 6,000 → INV-12 partial (₹4,000 left).

## 10.5 Paying a supplier (Make Payment)
Make Payment → supplier → purchase bills → amount → Dr Supplier (2101) / Cr cash-bank.

## 10.6 Advance & on-account
"Extra amount (no bill)" → advance (before the bill) or On Account (uncertain).

## 10.7 Customer ledger to zero
A credit sale debits the customer; collection credits; fully collected = 0 → the ledger is clear. Outstanding is derived automatically from active collections.

## 10.8 Status & ageing
Status: outstanding/partial/settled. Bills Outstanding → age buckets 0–30/31–60/61–90/90+.

## 10.9 Inside the voucher
Vouchers also has bill-wise settlement tiles.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Not linking the collection to a bill | Receive Payment |
| Not choosing a customer | The correct party |
| Advance as income | The customer’s deposit |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Receipts are adjusted against bills; dues are bill-wise; ageing analysis is available."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
Bill-wise records and ageing for debtors/creditors; special attention at 90+ days.

## ✅ Worked example
Ram ₹10,000 on credit (INV-12); ₹6,000 then ₹4,000 collected → outstanding → partial → settled; Ram’s ledger 0.

## 📚 Case study
All collections in a "general receipt" → no idea which bills remained. Adopting bill-wise made it clear.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. One receipt against several bills?** Yes.
**Q. More than the outstanding?** Into On Account/advance.
