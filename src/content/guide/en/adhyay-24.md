# Chapter 24 — Year-End Process & FY-Lock

> **After this chapter you will be able to:** the year-end checklist; the closing-stock journal; FY-Lock; carrying balances forward.

## 24.1 Why year-end
On 31 March the books are closed, the result is fixed, and the next year’s opening is created.

## 24.2 Checklist

| ✅ | Task |
|---|---|
| 1 | All vouchers entered and approved |
| 2 | Bank reconciliation |
| 3 | Physical stock count and reconciliation |
| 4 | Depreciation journal |
| 5 | Check dues/advances |
| 6 | Deposit TDS/GST/EPF payable |
| 7 | Trial balance Dr = Cr |
| 8 | Review final accounts |
| 9 | Profit appropriation |
| 10 | FY-Lock |

## 24.3 Closing-stock journal
Journal the closing stock to a dedicated account (5150/3400) — so Trading/Balance Sheet share one value.

## 24.4 FY-Lock
Society Setup → FY-Lock → the year is audit-locked. Then every mutation is blocked (RULE 6): "FY Locked — no changes."

> ⚠️ Make everything final before locking — after the lock, no changes.

## 24.5 Carrying balances forward
The closing balances become the next year’s opening — no re-entry.

## ⚠️ Common mistakes

| Mistake | Avoid by |
|---|---|
| Skipping stock reconciliation | A physical count |
| Outstanding taxes payable | Deposit first |
| Locking too early | Review first |

## 🔍 Audit note
"Year-end process complete; closing stock journalled; unchanged after FY-Lock."

## 📘 Cooperative standard
The year is locked after audit; changes to a prior year are forbidden.

## ✅ Worked example
Rania: all vouchers ✓, bank reconciled, stock found 0, depreciation journalled, trial balance balanced, appropriation done, then FY-Lock.

## 📚 Case study
After FY-Lock, an attempt to change an old voucher was blocked — the audit integrity was preserved.

## ❓ FAQ
**Q. Can the lock be opened?** Only by an Admin, carefully; normally not.
**Q. The next year after the lock?** Yes, work continues in the new FY.
