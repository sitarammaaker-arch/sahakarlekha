# KI-000044 · Prudence / Conservatism (प्रूडेंस)

**Definition:** Prudence (conservatism) is the accounting convention of being cautious — not overstating income or assets, and recognising likely losses and liabilities as soon as they are foreseen, so the accounts do not paint too rosy a picture.

**Plain-language explanation:** When unsure, lean to the safe side: don't count income before it is sure, but do provide for likely losses early.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** प्रूडेंस (prudence) यानी सतर्क रहना — आय या एसेट को बढ़ा-चढ़ाकर न दिखाना, और संभावित हानि व लायबिलिटी का अनुमान होते ही उसे दर्ज कर लेना। इससे खाते ज़रूरत से ज़्यादा अच्छे नहीं दिखते, बल्कि सच्ची व सुरक्षित तस्वीर देते हैं।

**English explanation:** A convention of caution: recognise expected losses early, do not anticipate gains — keeping the accounts realistic and reliable.

**Why it matters:** It protects members and auditors from over-optimistic accounts — e.g. providing for doubtful dues rather than assuming everything will be recovered.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Prudence means understating everything." → It means caution, not deliberate understatement.
- ❌ "You record gains as soon as you expect them." → Gains are recognised when realised, not merely expected.
