# KI-000082 · Account Group (अकाउंट ग्रुप)

**Definition:** An account group is a category that bundles related ledger accounts together — for example "Assets", "Liabilities", "Income", "Expenses", or a sub-group like "Bank Accounts" — so reports can total and present them in a structured way.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is a folder for accounts: many individual accounts sit under one group, and reports add up by group.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** अकाउंट ग्रुप वह श्रेणी है जो आपस में जुड़े लेजर खातों को एक साथ रखती है — जैसे "एसेट", "लायबिलिटी", "आय", "व्यय", या उप-समूह "बैंक खाते"। इससे रिपोर्ट खातों को समूह-वार जोड़कर व्यवस्थित ढंग से दिखाती है।

**English explanation:** A grouping layer in the chart of accounts that organises individual accounts under headings, enabling structured totals in reports.

**Why it matters:** Groups give the balance sheet and trial balance their structure (assets vs liabilities, etc.). Correct grouping = correct, readable reports.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Group and account are the same." → A group contains many accounts.
- ❌ "Grouping doesn't affect reports." → Reports total and present by group, so grouping shapes them.
