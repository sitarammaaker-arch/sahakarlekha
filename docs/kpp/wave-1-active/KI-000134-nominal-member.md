# KI-000134 · Nominal Member (नाममात्र सदस्य)

**Definition:** A nominal member is a person admitted for limited purposes (such as availing a specific service) who generally does not enjoy full membership rights like voting or sharing in surplus.

**Plain-language explanation:** A nominal member is a "limited" member — allowed to use certain services but usually without voting rights or a share in profit.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** नाममात्र सदस्य वह होता है जिसे किसी सीमित उद्देश्य (जैसे कोई एक सेवा लेने) के लिए जोड़ा जाता है, पर उसे आम तौर पर वोट या लाभ में हिस्सेदारी जैसे पूरे अधिकार नहीं मिलते। आसान शब्दों में — "सीमित अधिकार वाला सदस्य"।

**English explanation:** A limited-rights category of member, contrasted with a regular member who holds full rights; the exact rights follow the society's byelaws.

**Why it matters:** Societies often record different member categories; distinguishing nominal from regular members keeps the member register and voting/benefit handling correct.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A nominal member can vote like a regular member." → Generally not; rights are limited.
- ❌ "Nominal members get a dividend." → Typically they do not share in surplus (per byelaws).
