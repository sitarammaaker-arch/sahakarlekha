# KI-000121 · Bank Statement (बैंक स्टेटमेंट)

**Definition:** A bank statement is the bank's own record of all transactions in a society's bank account over a period, showing deposits, withdrawals, charges, and the bank's closing balance.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the list the bank gives you of everything that happened in your account — used to check against the society's own bank book.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** बैंक स्टेटमेंट बैंक का अपना रिकॉर्ड है जिसमें एक अवधि के दौरान समिति के बैंक खाते की सारी जमा, निकासी, शुल्क और बैंक के अनुसार अंतिम शेष दिखते हैं। इसे समिति की बैंक बही से मिलाकर (reconcile) देखा जाता है। आसान शब्दों में — "बैंक की तरफ़ से दिया गया हिसाब"।

**English explanation:** The bank-issued record of account activity; compared against the society's bank book during reconciliation to explain any differences.

**Why it matters:** It is the independent source used for bank reconciliation — a core check that the society's bank records are correct and complete.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "The statement balance must equal the book balance exactly at all times." → Timing differences (uncleared cheques, etc.) are normal and resolved by reconciliation.
- ❌ "The bank statement replaces the bank book." → Both are kept; one is the bank's record, the other the society's.
