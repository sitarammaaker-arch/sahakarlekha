# KI-000021 · Cooperative vs Company (सहकारी समिति बनाम कंपनी)

**Definition:** A cooperative differs from a company in ownership and purpose: a cooperative is member-owned and democratically controlled (one member, one vote) to serve members, whereas a company is owned by shareholders in proportion to capital and primarily aims to maximise shareholder profit.

**Plain-language explanation:** In a cooperative, control follows people (one vote each) and the goal is member benefit; in a company, control follows money (more shares, more votes) and the goal is investor profit.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** सबसे बड़ा फर्क — सहकारी समिति में नियंत्रण "लोगों" के पास होता है (हर सदस्य का एक वोट) और मकसद सदस्यों की भलाई होता है; जबकि कंपनी में नियंत्रण "पैसे" के पास होता है (जितने ज़्यादा शेयर, उतना ज़्यादा वोट) और मकसद निवेशकों का लाभ बढ़ाना होता है।

**English explanation:** Two different organisational forms — cooperative (people-centred, democratic, mutual benefit) vs company (capital-centred, profit-maximising) — with different laws, governance, and surplus treatment.

**Why it matters:** It explains why cooperative accounting, audit, and governance (AGM, reserves, dividend limits) look different from a private company's — a frequent source of confusion for new users.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Cooperatives can't make a surplus." → They can; it's just shared/retained by cooperative rules.
- ❌ "Bigger investment buys more control." → True for companies, not cooperatives.
