# KI-000116 · Cheque (चेक)

**Definition:** A cheque is a written instruction to a bank to pay a stated amount from the drawer's account to a named person or party; it is a common non-cash way for societies to receive and make payments.

**Plain-language explanation:** A cheque is a paper instruction telling the bank to pay a certain amount to someone from your account.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** चेक एक लिखित निर्देश है जो बैंक को कहता है कि खाते से तय रकम किसी व्यक्ति/पक्ष को दे दी जाए। समितियाँ अक्सर बिना नकद के भुगतान देने-लेने के लिए चेक का इस्तेमाल करती हैं। आसान शब्दों में — "बैंक को भुगतान का पर्चा"।

**English explanation:** A bank payment instrument used for non-cash receipts and payments; it clears through the banking system and affects the bank book.

**Why it matters:** Cheques are everyday bank instruments; knowing how they work supports correct bank entries and reconciliation (e.g. issued-but-not-cleared cheques).

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Writing a cheque instantly reduces the bank balance." → The book entry is made, but the bank balance changes only when the cheque clears — a reconciliation point.
- ❌ "A cheque is the same as cash." → It is a payment instruction, subject to clearing and possible dishonour.
