# KI-000113 · Bank Account (बैंक खाता)

**Definition:** A bank account is an account the society holds with a bank; in accounting it is an asset account tracking all money deposited into and withdrawn from that bank.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the society's money kept in a bank — and the matching account in the books that records every deposit and withdrawal.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** बैंक खाता वह खाता है जो समिति किसी बैंक में रखती है। लेखांकन में यह एक परिसंपत्ति खाता है, जिसमें उस बैंक में जमा और निकासी सब दर्ज होती है। आसान शब्दों में — बैंक में रखा समिति का पैसा और बही में उसका हिसाब।

**English explanation:** A society's account at a bank, mirrored by an asset ledger account that records deposits and withdrawals for that bank.

**Why it matters:** Most society money moves through banks; a correct bank account (and reconciliation) is central to trustworthy books. A society may hold several bank accounts.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Bank balance in books always equals the passbook." → They can differ temporarily; reconciliation explains the difference.
- ❌ "All banks share one account in the books." → Each bank account is tracked separately.
