# KI-000046 · Books of Account (लेखा-बही)

**Definition:** Books of account are the records in which a society keeps its financial transactions — primarily the cash book, bank book, day book, journal, and ledger — forming the basis for its reports and audit.

**Plain-language explanation:** They are the society's official "registers" where all money entries live — the source from which every report and audit is built.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** लेखा-बही वे रिकॉर्ड हैं जिनमें समिति अपने सभी लेन-देन रखती है — मुख्यतः रोकड़ बही, बैंक बही, रोज़नामचा (day book), जर्नल और खाता बही (ledger)। इन्हीं से रिपोर्ट और ऑडिट तैयार होते हैं। आसान शब्दों में — समिति की "हिसाब की किताबें"।

**English explanation:** The set of primary records (cash/bank/day book, journal, ledger) that capture transactions and feed the financial statements.

**Why it matters:** Clean, complete books are the precondition for correct reports and a smooth audit — the whole point of using SahakarLekha.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "One register is enough." → Different books serve different purposes (cash, bank, ledger, etc.).
- ❌ "Books of account and final accounts are the same." → Books are the day-to-day records; final accounts are the year-end summaries built from them.
