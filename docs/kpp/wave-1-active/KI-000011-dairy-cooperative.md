# KI-000011 · Dairy Cooperative Society (दुग्ध सहकारी समिति)

**Definition:** A dairy cooperative society is a society of milk producers that collects members' milk, tests it for quality, pays members for it, and markets it (often through a district/state milk union) — bringing fair prices and a steady market to small dairy farmers.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the village milk society — members pour their milk daily, it is weighed and tested, and members are paid based on quantity and quality.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** दुग्ध सहकारी समिति दूध उत्पादकों की समिति है जो सदस्यों का दूध इकट्ठा करती है, उसकी गुणवत्ता (फैट/SNF) जाँचती है, उसका भुगतान करती है और आगे (जिला/राज्य दुग्ध संघ के ज़रिए) बेचती है। इससे छोटे डेयरी किसानों को उचित दाम और पक्का बाज़ार मिलता है।

**English explanation:** A milk-producers' cooperative that pools, tests, pays for, and markets members' milk — the grassroots unit of the cooperative dairy structure (e.g. the Amul model).

**Why it matters:** Dairy societies are a major SahakarLekha segment; their accounting centres on daily milk collection, fat/SNF-based payments, and dues — patterns the software supports.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A dairy society only sells milk." → Many also supply cattle feed, veterinary services, and inputs to members.
- ❌ "All members are paid the same rate." → Payment usually depends on quantity and quality (fat/SNF). *(Exact rate treatment is a separate, validation-needed topic.)*
