# KI-000034 · Asset (परिसंपत्ति)

**Definition:** An asset is anything of value the society owns or is owed — such as cash, bank balances, loans given to members, stock, buildings, or equipment — that can provide future benefit.

**Plain-language explanation:** Assets are what the society *has* or *will receive* — its money, things, and amounts due to it.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** परिसंपत्ति वह सब है जो समिति के पास है या जो समिति को मिलना है — जैसे रोकड़, बैंक में जमा, सदस्यों को दिया गया कर्ज़, माल (स्टॉक), इमारत, उपकरण आदि। सीधे शब्दों में — समिति की "जमा-पूँजी वाली चीज़ें" जिनसे आगे लाभ मिलेगा।

**English explanation:** Resources owned or receivable by the society that carry future economic benefit; one of the main sides of the balance sheet.

**Why it matters:** Assets are half the balance sheet and a core account category. Classifying items correctly as assets keeps reports accurate.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Only cash is an asset." → Loans receivable, stock, and fixed items are assets too.
- ❌ "Assets are always physical." → Amounts owed to the society (receivables) are assets without being physical.
