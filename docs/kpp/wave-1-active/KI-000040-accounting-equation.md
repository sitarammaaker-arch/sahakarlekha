# KI-000040 · Accounting Equation (अकाउंटिंग इक्वेशन)

**Definition:** The accounting equation states that **Assets = Liabilities + Capital** — meaning everything a society owns is funded either by what it owes to others or by members' own funds.

**Plain-language explanation:** Whatever the society *has* (assets) must equal what it *owes* (liabilities) plus what *belongs to members* (capital). The two sides always match.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** अकाउंटिंग इक्वेशन कहता है — **एसेट = लायबिलिटी + कैपिटल**। यानी समिति के पास जो कुछ है (एसेट), वह या तो दूसरों के पैसे से आया है (लायबिलिटी) या सदस्यों के अपने धन से (कैपिटल)। इसीलिए बैलेंस शीट के दोनों पक्ष हमेशा बराबर होते हैं।

**English explanation:** The fundamental balance that underlies double-entry and the balance sheet: total assets always equal total liabilities plus capital.

**Why it matters:** It is *why* the balance sheet balances and *why* double-entry works. If it doesn't tally, something is wrong.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "The equation only matters at year-end." → It holds true after every single transaction.
- ❌ "Capital and liabilities are the same." → Capital is members' own funds; liabilities are owed to outsiders/depositors.
