# KI-000053 · Accounting Period (लेखांकन अवधि)

**Definition:** An accounting period is the span of time for which accounts are drawn up and results measured — usually the financial year, but it can also be a month or quarter for internal review.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the chunk of time the accounts cover — most often the full financial year, but you can also look at a month or quarter.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** लेखांकन अवधि वह समय-खंड है जिसके लिए खाते बनाए जाते हैं और परिणाम मापे जाते हैं — आम तौर पर वित्तीय वर्ष, पर आंतरिक समीक्षा के लिए महीना या तिमाही भी हो सकती है।

**English explanation:** The period (typically the FY) over which performance is measured and statements are prepared, enabling comparison across periods.

**Why it matters:** Defining the period lets income and expenses be matched to the right span, so results and comparisons are meaningful. Reports in SahakarLekha are always tied to a period.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Accounting period must be a full year." → It is usually a year, but shorter periods are used for reviews.
- ❌ "Period and financial year are unrelated." → The financial year is the standard accounting period.
