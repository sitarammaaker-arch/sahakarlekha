# KI-000135 · Associate Member (सहयोगी सदस्य)

**Definition:** An associate member is a member admitted (often jointly with a main member, or for limited purposes) who generally holds restricted rights — for example, they may not have independent voting rights — as defined by the society's byelaws.

**Plain-language explanation:** An associate member is a "secondary" member with limited rights, often linked to a main member.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** सहयोगी सदस्य वह होता है जिसे (अक्सर किसी मुख्य सदस्य के साथ संयुक्त रूप से, या सीमित उद्देश्य के लिए) जोड़ा जाता है और जिसके अधिकार आम तौर पर सीमित होते हैं — जैसे स्वतंत्र वोट का अधिकार न हो। ये अधिकार समिति की उपविधि से तय होते हैं।

**English explanation:** A limited-rights member category, distinct from a regular member; exact rights follow the byelaws.

**Why it matters:** Societies record different member categories; classifying associate vs regular members keeps the register and voting/benefit handling correct.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Associate and nominal members are identical." → Both are limited-rights categories but defined differently by byelaws.
- ❌ "An associate member always votes." → Voting rights are typically restricted.
