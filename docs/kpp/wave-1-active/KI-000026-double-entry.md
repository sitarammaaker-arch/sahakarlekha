# KI-000026 · Double-Entry System (डबल एंट्री सिस्टम)

**Definition:** Double-entry is the bookkeeping system in which every transaction is recorded in at least two accounts — one debit and one credit of equal amount — so the books always stay balanced.

**Plain-language explanation:** Every entry has two sides: money comes from somewhere and goes somewhere. Recording both sides keeps the accounts in balance and makes errors easy to catch.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** डबल एंट्री सिस्टम में हर लेन-देन कम से कम दो खातों में लिखा जाता है — एक तरफ़ "नाम" (Debit) और दूसरी तरफ़ बराबर रकम का "जमा" (Credit)। इससे हिसाब हमेशा बराबर (balance) रहता है और गलती पकड़ना आसान हो जाता है। हर रकम कहीं से आती है और कहीं जाती है — दोनों लिखो।

**English explanation:** The principle that for every transaction, total debits equal total credits, keeping the accounting equation balanced and the books self-checking.

**Why it matters:** It is the backbone of reliable accounting. SahakarLekha enforces it automatically, which is why the trial balance tallies (Dr = Cr).

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Double-entry means writing the same thing twice." → It means recording two *different* sides (debit and credit) of one transaction.
- ❌ "It is only needed in software." → It is the universal accounting principle, software just automates it.
