# KI-000153 · Share (शेयर / शेयर)

**Definition:** A share is a unit of a member's contribution to the society's capital; holding at least one share is usually a condition of membership and represents the member's ownership stake.

**Plain-language explanation:** A share is the basic "ownership unit" a member buys to join — it shows the member has put money into the society's capital.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** शेयर समिति की कैपिटल में सदस्य के योगदान की एक इकाई है। आम तौर पर कम से कम एक शेयर रखना सदस्यता की शर्त होती है, और यह सदस्य के स्वामित्व-हिस्से को दर्शाता है। आसान शब्दों में — "समिति में सदस्य की मालिकाना इकाई"।

**English explanation:** The unit of member capital contribution; ownership in a cooperative is held in shares, though control stays one-member-one-vote regardless of shares held.

**Why it matters:** Shares are the basis of share capital, the share register, and (where applicable) dividends — core member-and-capital records in SahakarLekha.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "More shares = more votes." → In cooperatives, voting is one member, one vote, irrespective of shares.
- ❌ "Cooperative shares trade on a stock market." → They do not; they are held and transferred per society rules.
