# KI-000117 · Demand Draft (डिमांड ड्राफ्ट)

**Definition:** A demand draft (DD) is a prepaid payment instrument issued by a bank, guaranteeing payment of a stated amount to a named party — used when a guaranteed, non-cash payment is required (unlike a cheque, the funds are paid upfront to the bank).

**Plain-language explanation:** A DD is a "guaranteed cheque" — you pay the bank first, and it issues a draft that the receiver can be sure will clear.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** डिमांड ड्राफ्ट (DD) बैंक द्वारा जारी किया गया पूर्व-भुगतान साधन है जो तय रकम के भुगतान की गारंटी देता है। चेक के विपरीत इसमें पैसा पहले ही बैंक को दे दिया जाता है, इसलिए पाने वाले को भरोसा रहता है कि यह ज़रूर मिलेगा। गारंटीशुदा भुगतान के लिए इसका उपयोग होता है।

**English explanation:** A bank-guaranteed prepaid draft for non-cash payment; recorded through the bank book like other bank transactions.

**Why it matters:** Societies use DDs for secure payments (e.g. to departments/suppliers). Recording them correctly keeps the bank book accurate.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A DD and a cheque are the same." → A cheque can bounce; a DD is prepaid and guaranteed.
- ❌ "A DD needs no entry." → It is a bank transaction and must be recorded.
