# KI-000102 · Cash-in-Hand (हस्तगत रोकड़)

**Definition:** Cash-in-hand is the amount of physical cash the society actually holds at a given moment — the closing balance of the cash book — which should match the notes and coins physically counted.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is how much cash the society has right now. The cash book's balance should equal the cash you can physically count.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** हस्तगत रोकड़ वह नकद है जो समिति के पास किसी समय वास्तव में मौजूद है — यानी रोकड़ बही का समापन शेष। यह भौतिक रूप से गिने गए नोट-सिक्कों से मेल खाना चाहिए। अंतर हो तो गड़बड़ी का संकेत है।

**English explanation:** The physical cash balance per the cash book at any time; verifying it against a physical count is a basic, important control.

**Why it matters:** Cash-in-hand is a frequent audit check. If the book balance and physical cash differ, it signals an error or leakage to fix immediately.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Cash-in-hand includes the bank balance." → Bank money is separate; cash-in-hand is physical cash only.
- ❌ "It can be negative." → Physical cash cannot be negative; a negative balance means an error.
