# KI-000024 · Cooperative Lifecycle (सहकारी समिति का जीवन-चक्र)

**Definition:** The cooperative lifecycle is the sequence of stages a society passes through — formation and registration, day-to-day operation, annual audit and AGM, growth, and (if needed) dissolution — each with its own duties and records.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the "life story" of a society: it is formed and registered, runs and serves members, gets audited and holds its AGM each year, grows, and eventually may close.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** सहकारी समिति का जीवन-चक्र वे चरण हैं जिनसे समिति गुज़रती है — गठन व पंजीकरण, रोज़मर्रा का संचालन, हर साल ऑडिट व वार्षिक आम सभा (AGM), विकास, और ज़रूरत पड़ने पर विघटन। हर चरण की अपनी ज़िम्मेदारियाँ और रिकॉर्ड होते हैं।

**English explanation:** The stages from registration through operation, audit/AGM, growth, and dissolution — a map of what a society does over its life.

**Why it matters:** Seeing the lifecycle shows *when* each task (registration, yearly audit, AGM, year-end close) happens — and where SahakarLekha helps at each stage.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A society just runs once registered." → Each year brings recurring duties (audit, AGM, returns).
- ❌ "Closing a society is informal." → Dissolution follows a defined legal process.
