# KI-000061 · Narration (विवरण)

**Definition:** Narration is the short note attached to a voucher that explains the transaction in plain words — what it was for, to/from whom, and any reference — so anyone reading the entry later understands it.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the one-line "why" written with each entry, so the transaction makes sense to anyone who reads it months later.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** विवरण (narration) हर वाउचर के साथ लिखी गई छोटी टिप्पणी है जो लेन-देन को सरल शब्दों में समझाती है — किसलिए, किससे/किसको, और कोई संदर्भ (बिल/चेक नंबर)। इससे बाद में एंट्री पढ़ने वाला तुरंत समझ जाता है कि वह किस बारे में थी।

**English explanation:** The explanatory note on a voucher; a clear narration makes the books self-explanatory and audit-friendly.

**Why it matters:** Good narration is the difference between books anyone can follow and a pile of cryptic entries. Auditors and future staff rely on it.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Narration is optional fluff." → It is what makes an entry understandable and auditable.
- ❌ "One-word narration is enough." → A clear short sentence (purpose + party + reference) is far more useful.
