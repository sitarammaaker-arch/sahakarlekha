# KI-000012 · Consumer Cooperative Society (उपभोक्ता सहकारी समिति)

**Definition:** A consumer cooperative society buys goods in bulk and sells them to its members (and sometimes the public) at fair prices — running a shop/store owned by the consumers themselves to cut out excess margins.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is a members' shop: the society buys goods wholesale and sells them to members at honest prices, and any surplus benefits the members.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** उपभोक्ता सहकारी समिति थोक में सामान खरीदकर अपने सदस्यों (और कभी-कभी आम जनता) को उचित दाम पर बेचती है। यह उपभोक्ताओं की अपनी दुकान होती है, जिससे बिचौलियों का अतिरिक्त मुनाफ़ा घटता है और लाभ सदस्यों को मिलता है।

**English explanation:** A retail cooperative owned by its consumer-members, supplying everyday goods at fair prices; its books revolve around stock, sales, and (often) GST.

**Why it matters:** Consumer societies use SahakarLekha's inventory, sales, and GST features heavily; understanding the type guides correct setup.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A consumer society can only serve members." → Many also sell to the public, per byelaws.
- ❌ "It must always make a big profit." → Its goal is fair prices to members, not maximum profit.
