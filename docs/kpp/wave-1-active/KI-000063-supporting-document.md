# KI-000063 · Supporting Document / Bill (समर्थक दस्तावेज़)

**Definition:** A supporting document is the proof attached to a voucher — such as a bill, receipt, invoice, or voucher slip — that evidences the transaction actually happened and for the stated amount.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the paper (or scan) behind an entry — the bill or receipt that proves the transaction is real.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** समर्थक दस्तावेज़ वह सबूत है जो वाउचर के साथ जोड़ा जाता है — जैसे बिल, रसीद, इनवॉइस या पर्ची — जो दिखाता है कि लेन-देन वाकई हुआ और तय रकम का हुआ। ऑडिट में यही प्रमाण माँगा जाता है।

**English explanation:** The evidence (bill/receipt/invoice) backing a voucher; keeping it attached makes the entry verifiable and audit-ready.

**Why it matters:** Entries without proof are a common audit objection. Attaching supporting documents protects the society and speeds up audit.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "The voucher alone is enough." → The voucher is the entry; the bill/receipt is the proof.
- ❌ "Small payments need no proof." → Even small payments are best supported by a receipt.
