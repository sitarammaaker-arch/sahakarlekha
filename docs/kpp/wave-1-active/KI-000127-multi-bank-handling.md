# KI-000127 · Multi-Bank Handling (कई बैंक खातों का प्रबंधन)

**Definition:** Multi-bank handling is the ability to maintain several bank accounts in the books at once — each with its own bank book, balance, and reconciliation — which most societies need as they bank with more than one bank/branch.

**Plain-language explanation:** A society often has more than one bank account; the software tracks each one separately, with its own balance and reconciliation.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** कई बैंक खातों का प्रबंधन यानी बही में एक साथ कई बैंक खाते रखना — हर एक की अपनी बैंक बही, शेष और मिलान (reconciliation)। अधिकांश समितियों के एक से ज़्यादा बैंक/शाखा में खाते होते हैं, इसलिए यह ज़रूरी है।

**English explanation:** Tracking multiple bank accounts independently in one set of books — each reconciled on its own — which SahakarLekha supports.

**Why it matters:** Mixing banks into one account hides errors. Keeping each bank separate makes balances and reconciliation accurate.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "All banks can share one account in the books." → Each bank account is tracked separately.
- ❌ "Only big societies have multiple banks." → Even small societies often have 2+ accounts.
