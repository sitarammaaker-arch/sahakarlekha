# KI-000099 · Cash (रोकड़ / नकद)

**Definition:** Cash is the physical money (notes and coins) the society holds and can use immediately; in accounting it is an asset tracked through the cash account and cash book.

**Plain-language explanation:** Cash is the actual money in hand — the notes and coins the society physically has right now.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** रोकड़ यानी समिति के पास मौजूद असली नकद पैसा (नोट और सिक्के) जो तुरंत इस्तेमाल हो सकता है। लेखांकन में यह एक परिसंपत्ति है, जिसे रोकड़ खाता और रोकड़ बही से ट्रैक किया जाता है। आसान शब्दों में — "हाथ में मौजूद नकद"।

**English explanation:** Physical money held by the society — an immediately usable current asset recorded in the cash account/cash book.

**Why it matters:** Cash handling is where errors and audit issues commonly arise; understanding cash as a tracked asset is the basis for the cash book and verification.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Cash includes bank balance." → Bank money is separate (the bank account); cash is physical money in hand.
- ❌ "Cash and income are the same." → Cash is an asset you hold; income is what you earn.
