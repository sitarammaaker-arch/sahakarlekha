# KI-000120 · Passbook (पासबुक)

**Definition:** A passbook is the bank-issued record of a society's account transactions and balance, printed/updated by the bank — the bank's own version of the account, used to cross-check the society's bank book.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the booklet (or statement) the bank gives showing your account's deposits, withdrawals, and balance — used to match against your own bank book.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** पासबुक बैंक द्वारा दी गई वह पुस्तिका/रिकॉर्ड है जिसमें समिति के खाते की जमा, निकासी और शेष दर्ज होते हैं। यह बैंक का अपना संस्करण है, जिसे समिति की बैंक बही से मिलाकर (reconcile) जाँचा जाता है।

**English explanation:** The bank's printed record of the account; like a bank statement, it is compared with the society's bank book during reconciliation.

**Why it matters:** The passbook/statement is the independent source for bank reconciliation — a core check that the society's records are correct.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "The passbook is the society's own record." → It is the bank's record; the society keeps its bank book.
- ❌ "Passbook and bank book must match exactly at all times." → Timing differences are normal and explained by reconciliation.
