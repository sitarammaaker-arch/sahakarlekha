# KI-000037 · Income (आय)

**Definition:** Income is the money a society earns from its activities — such as interest on loans, commission, sale of goods, fees, or service charges — that increases its funds during a period.

**Plain-language explanation:** Income is what comes *in* as earnings — the money the society makes from what it does.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** आय वह धन है जो समिति अपने कामकाज से कमाती है — जैसे कर्ज़ पर ब्याज, कमीशन, माल की बिक्री, शुल्क या सेवा प्रभार। यह एक अवधि में समिति के धन को बढ़ाती है। आसान शब्दों में — समिति की "कमाई"।

**English explanation:** Earnings from operations that increase the society's funds over an accounting period; shown in the Profit & Loss / Income & Expenditure account.

**Why it matters:** Income vs expense determines surplus or deficit. Classifying earnings correctly as income keeps the result and reports accurate.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "All money received is income." → Money received as a deposit or a loan is a liability, not income.
- ❌ "Income equals cash in hand." → Income is earnings; cash is a separate asset.
