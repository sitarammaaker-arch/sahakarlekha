# KI-000100 · Cash Account (रोकड़ खाता)

**Definition:** The cash account is the ledger account that tracks all cash received (debits) and cash paid (credits), with its balance representing the cash in hand.

**Plain-language explanation:** It is the single account where all cash movement is summed up — its balance is how much cash the society holds.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** रोकड़ खाता वह लेजर खाता है जिसमें सारी नकद प्राप्ति (Dr) और नकद भुगतान (Cr) दर्ज होती है, और जिसका शेष "हाथ में रोकड़" दिखाता है। आसान शब्दों में — नकद की हर हलचल को जोड़ने वाला एक खाता, जिसका बैलेंस ही उपलब्ध रोकड़ है।

**English explanation:** The asset account for cash; in SahakarLekha it underlies the cash book and always reflects cash in hand.

**Why it matters:** It connects the cash book to the ledger and trial balance; its balance must match physically counted cash during verification.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Cash account and bank account are one." → They are separate accounts.
- ❌ "A negative cash balance is normal." → Cash in hand cannot be negative; a negative balance signals an error.
