# KI-000050 · Financial Year (वित्तीय वर्ष)

**Definition:** A financial year (FY) is the 12-month period for which a society prepares its accounts; in India it normally runs from 1 April to 31 March of the next year.

**Plain-language explanation:** The financial year is the society's "accounting year" — the fixed 12 months over which income, expenses, and reports are measured.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** वित्तीय वर्ष वह 12 महीने की अवधि है जिसके लिए समिति अपने खाते बनाती है। भारत में यह आम तौर पर 1 अप्रैल से अगले साल 31 मार्च तक चलता है। इसी अवधि की आय, खर्च और रिपोर्ट तैयार होती हैं। आसान शब्दों में — समिति का "हिसाब वाला साल"।

**English explanation:** The standard 12-month accounting period (1 April–31 March in India) used to measure performance and prepare statements.

**Why it matters:** Every report, opening balance, and year-end close is tied to the FY. Choosing the right FY at setup keeps all reports aligned.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "Financial year = calendar year (Jan–Dec)." → In India it is usually April–March.
- ❌ "The FY can be changed mid-year freely." → It is fixed for the period; changes follow rules and proper closing.
