# KI-000038 · Expense (व्यय)

**Definition:** An expense is the money a society spends to run its activities — such as salaries, rent, interest paid, stationery, or repairs — that reduces its funds during a period.

**Plain-language explanation:** An expense is what goes *out* as a cost of running the society — the money spent to keep things working.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** व्यय वह धन है जो समिति अपने कामकाज को चलाने में खर्च करती है — जैसे वेतन, किराया, चुकाया गया ब्याज, स्टेशनरी या मरम्मत। यह एक अवधि में समिति के धन को घटाता है। आसान शब्दों में — समिति का "खर्च"।

**English explanation:** Costs incurred to operate the society that reduce funds over a period; shown in the Profit & Loss / Income & Expenditure account.

**Why it matters:** Expenses offset income to give surplus or deficit. Correct expense classification keeps results, budgets, and audits clean.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "All money paid is an expense." → Repaying a loan or giving a loan is not an expense (it changes a liability/asset).
- ❌ "Buying an asset is an expense." → Purchasing a long-life asset is capital expenditure, recorded as an asset, not a period expense.
