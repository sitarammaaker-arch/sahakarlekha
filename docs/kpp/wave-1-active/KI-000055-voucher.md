# KI-000055 · Voucher (वाउचर)

**Definition:** A voucher is the document/record that captures a single transaction in the books — showing its date, the accounts debited and credited, the amount, and a narration, usually backed by a supporting bill or receipt.

**Plain-language explanation:** A voucher is one recorded entry — the basic "slip" that says what happened, on which date, for how much, and which accounts it affected.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** वाउचर वह रिकॉर्ड है जिसमें एक लेन-देन दर्ज होता है — उसकी तारीख, किस खाते को "नाम" (Dr) और किसे "जमा" (Cr), कितनी रकम, और एक विवरण (narration)। आम तौर पर इसके साथ बिल/रसीद जुड़ी होती है। आसान शब्दों में — हर लेन-देन की "एंट्री पर्ची"।

**English explanation:** The unit of entry in accounting software: each transaction is recorded as a voucher with debit, credit, amount, date, and narration.

**Why it matters:** Vouchers are how data enters SahakarLekha. Every ledger, report, and audit trail is built from vouchers, so correct vouchers = correct everything.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A voucher is only the paper bill." → The bill is the *support*; the voucher is the accounting entry.
- ❌ "All vouchers are the same." → There are types (receipt, payment, journal, contra, compound) for different situations.
