# KI-000010 · PACS — Primary Agricultural Credit Society (प्राथमिक कृषि साख समिति)

**Definition:** A PACS is a village-level primary cooperative that provides short-term credit and related services (inputs, deposits, sometimes supply/PDS) to farmer-members, usually linked upward to a district central cooperative bank and the wider cooperative credit structure.

**Plain-language explanation:** A PACS is the local farmers' credit society in a village — the first point where a farmer gets a crop loan and basic services through the cooperative system.

**Hindi explanation (आसान हिंदी):** PACS यानी प्राथमिक कृषि साख समिति — गाँव स्तर की वह समिति जो किसानों को मुख्यतः अल्पकालीन (फसली) कर्ज़ और जुड़ी सेवाएँ देती है। यह आगे जिला सहकारी बैंक से जुड़ी होती है। किसान के लिए सहकारी ढाँचे का यह सबसे नज़दीकी हिस्सा है।

**English explanation:** The grassroots unit of the rural cooperative credit system — a village-level society serving farmer-members with short-term credit and allied services.

**Why it matters:** PACS are a huge share of India's cooperatives and a core SahakarLekha audience; their accounting centres on members, crop loans, and recovery.

**Common misconceptions:**
- ❌ "A PACS is a bank." → It is a primary society linked to the cooperative banking structure, not a bank itself.
- ❌ "PACS only give loans." → Many also handle deposits, inputs, and supply/PDS.
