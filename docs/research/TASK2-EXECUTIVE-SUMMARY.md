## 1. What Phase-2 established

Phase-2 mapped the **complete accounting, statutory, audit, tax, treasury and charting framework** governing the eight Indian cooperative types (PACS, Marketing, Consumer, Dairy, Housing, Labour, Industrial, Multi-State). Across all eight documents a **single coherent operating model** emerged: a cooperative is a member-owned entity running a defined business activity, keeping statutorily-prescribed books, closing annually on a fixed appropriation logic, submitting to mandatory external audit, and layering general tax/labour law on top of cooperative-specific law. The deliverables are internally consistent; the same load-bearing facts recur independently across domain, compliance, register, audit, workflow and framework lenses — which is itself corroboration.

## 2. Consolidated verified findings (High confidence — cross-document)

**F1 — Dual legal spine.** Single-state societies are governed by their **State Cooperative Societies Act + Rules + registered bye-laws** under the **State Registrar (RCS)**; multi-state societies by the **MSCS Act 2002 + Rules** under the **Central Registrar (CRCS)**. Credit/PACS/cooperative-bank societies carry an additional **NABARD/RBI** prudential overlay. This frame determines every downstream requirement.

**F2 — PACS run on NABARD's Common Accounting System (CAS).** Accrual basis, standardised general-ledger heads, prescribed books (cash book, day book, general/personal ledger, sales register, vouchers), NPA/prudential norms, and offline capability. CAS is the closest thing to a national cooperative accounting standard — but it is **PACS-specific**, not universal to the other seven types.

**F3 — A uniform surplus-appropriation order.** Net profit is appropriated in a fixed sequence: **statutory reserve (commonly ≥25%) → cooperative education fund → other bye-law funds → dividend (within a statutory cap) → carry-forward surplus.** This recurs identically across the domain, compliance, workflow and framework documents.

**F4 — Mandatory annual statutory audit is the central compliance event.** Conducted **within 6 months of financial-year close** by a Registrar-empanelled/departmental auditor or a Chartered Accountant (CA mandatory for MSCS); societies are graded **A/B/C/D**; the report goes to both the AGM and the Registrar, with a **rectification/follow-up report (~3 months of AGM adoption)**. Five audit modes exist: **statutory, internal, government inspection, special, and concurrent** (the last effectively confined to cooperative banks under RBI norms).

**F5 — A fixed governance/closing calendar.** Financial year **1 April–31 March**; final accounts prepared within ~45 days of close; **AGM by ~30 September** (within 6 months); **annual return to the Registrar within 6 months** (some rules: within 30 days of AGM); **records retained ≥10 years**. Year-end closing follows a standard sequence (cut-off → reconciliation → physical verification → accruals/provisions/NPA → closing & appropriation entries → statements).

**F6 — A statutory core register set, plus a practice tail.** Statutorily prescribed (Maharashtra Rule 65 as the reference): **minute books (general + committee), cash book, general & personal ledger, stock register, property register, audit-objection register**, plus **Member Register (Form I)** and **Share Register (Form J)**. A large secondary set (petty cash, cheque, advance, security-deposit, depreciation, procurement, godown, dead-stock, board-resolution, legal-case registers) is **industry/bye-law practice**, not directly statutory.

**F7 — A predictable tax & statutory-payroll overlay.** GST (registration above **₹40L goods / ₹20L services**; GSTR-3B by 20th); TDS (deposit by **7th**, quarterly returns); income tax (**s.80P** deduction for eligible societies; **s.115BAD** flat 22% but excludes 80P; AMT 15%; **ITR 31 Oct** for audited); payroll statutory (**EPF at 20+ employees, ESI at 10+/wages ≤₹21,000, both remitted by 15th**); **professional tax** state-specific (not levied in all states). A monthly/quarterly/half-yearly/annual compliance cadence is well-defined.

**F8 — A consistent internal-control architecture.** Cash-holding limits + daily cash verification; **monthly signed bank reconciliation**; joint bank signatories; **maker-checker** for electronic payments; permitted-securities discipline for investments; **fund accounting with backing investment** (esp. housing sinking/repair funds); and a role-based **financial authorization matrix** with segregation of duties. Controls operate on a daily/weekly/monthly/year-end cadence.

**F9 — A generic, class-coded Chart of Accounts is derivable** (Assets / Liabilities / Equity-Own-Funds / Income / Expenses), with cooperative-specific heads: member-wise share capital, statutory/sinking/repair/education funds, member deposits (SB/FD/RD/pigmy/thrift), member loans (ST/MT/LT), DCB/NPA provisioning, per-category sales/purchase routing, and appropriation accounts. Control accounts reconcile to member-wise subsidiary ledgers.

**F10 — Type-specific specifics that are firmly established.** Dairy: **Fat/SNF two-axis milk pricing** with milk-collection and fat-test registers; a thin pass-through margin. Housing (Maharashtra): **sinking fund min 0.25%/annum of construction cost, repair ~0.75%, transfer premium capped ₹25,000**, Forms I/J. Credit/PACS: loan register + **DCB register** + NPA classification as the core discipline. Marketing: **pool accounting** and agency/NAFED settlement. Labour: statutory **muster roll & wage registers**, wages by 7th.

**F11 — The recurring audit-defect cluster is credit-centric.** The highest-frequency, highest-impact objections concentrate on **loan overdues, NPA misclassification/under-provisioning, and overdue interest wrongly booked as income** — followed by documentation/voucher gaps, non-reconciliation, and stock shortages. This is the single most important risk pattern surfaced.

## 4. Research gaps (not covered by Phase-2)

1. **Multi-state legal depth.** Only Maharashtra + central acts (MSCS) + NABARD were primary-sourced. A state-by-state comparative of the ~28 state cooperative acts was **out of scope** and remains the largest gap.
2. **Sector standards not deep-dived.** Sugar cooperatives, fisheries, and handloom/weaver societies were treated only at the "industrial cooperative" high level; FPOs (covered in Phase-1) were not revisited here.
3. **Actual statutory formats not reproduced.** Receipts & Payments, DCB, Trading/P&L, Balance Sheet and CAS formats were described **structurally**, not reproduced as fillable formats with line items.
4. **Cooperative-bank (UCB/DCCB/StCB) accounting** was covered only at overview level; RBI prudential/CBS-grade accounting detail is a separate body of work.
5. **Case-law & sub-clause detail** for s.80P eligibility, dividend-cap computation, and mutuality (housing) was not researched.
6. **No field validation.** All findings are desk research from official texts and reputable secondary sources; **no practitioner interviews or live society-book samples** were used.
7. **Link to Phase-1 not drawn.** The PACS-ERP/NLPS computerization wave (Phase-1) and its operational effect on these accounting workflows was deliberately not connected (research-only, per-phase scope).
