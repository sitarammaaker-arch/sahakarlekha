**Governing-law key (applies throughout):** Single-state societies → the relevant **State Cooperative Societies Act & Rules + registered bye-laws**, administered by the **State Registrar of Cooperative Societies (RCS)**. Societies operating across >1 state → **Multi-State Co-operative Societies (MSCS) Act 2002 & Rules**, administered by the **Central Registrar (CRCS)**. Credit/PACS/cooperative-bank societies additionally follow **NABARD/RBI** norms. **Exact section numbers, forms, frequencies and percentages vary by state act/rules** — items resting on a single state (usually Maharashtra) or on general practice are flagged **[NV per state]**.

## Topic notes (nuance & Needs-Verification detail)

**1. State Coop Acts — accounting requirements.** Prescribe books of account, statutory funds (**Reserve ≥25% net profit** — common baseline), Co-op Education Fund, dividend ceiling, surplus appropriation order, and mandatory annual audit. Exact percentages/caps are state-specific **[NV per state]**.

**2. MSCS Act 2002.** Federal-registered societies file with the **Central Registrar**; audit by a **Chartered Accountant**; annual return within 6 months of FY close (s.120); inspection under **Chapter VIII**.

**4. Statutory Audit.** Mandatory annually for every registered society; auditor is a **Registrar-empanelled/departmental auditor or CA** (CA required for MSCS). Audit completed **within 6 months** of FY close; audit report goes to the AGM **and** the Registrar; **board must file a follow-up/rectification report to the Registrar (commonly within 3 months of AGM acceptance)**. Societies are graded **A/B/C/D** — criteria per state audit manual **[NV per state]**.

**5. Internal Audit.** Statutorily required for **cooperative banks and large societies/federations** (RBI/NABARD/state rules); for small societies it is bye-law-driven/optional. Turnover/deposit thresholds vary **[NV per state/sector]**.

**6 & 9. Annual Returns / AGM.** AGM **within 6 months of FY close (commonly by 30 September)**; some state rules also require returns filed **within 30 days of the AGM**. Non-holding of AGM empowers the Registrar to convene it.

**7. Mandatory Registers (typical core set).** Register of Members; Register of Shares; Minute books (general body + committee); Cash book; General & personal ledgers; Register of Investments; Register of Loans (lending societies); Stock register (trading societies); Fixed-asset register; Register of audit objections/rectification. Full prescribed list is state-specific **[NV per state]**.

**11. Cash Book.** Core statutory book; written up **daily** with cash-balance verification. For PACS it follows the **NABARD Common Accounting System (CAS)**.

**12 & 13. Share / Member Registers.** In Maharashtra these are **Form J (shares)** and **Form I / Rule 32 (members)**; form names/numbers differ by state **[NV per state]**. Member and share records are effectively permanent.

**15/16/17. Stock / Investment / Loan Registers.** Applicability is activity-driven: **Stock** for trading types (PACS, marketing, consumer, dairy, industrial); **Loan** for credit types (PACS, credit/thrift, MSCS credit, coop banks) with NABARD/RBI NPA norms; **Investment** wherever surplus is deployed in permitted securities.

**18 & 19. Vouchers / Retention.** Vouchers support every entry and are preserved for the statutory retention period — **commonly ≥10 years** of books + vouchers before the current FY (varies by state; some registers effectively permanent) **[NV per state]**.

**20. Inspection.** Not routine — triggered by Registrar order, complaint, creditor, or a threshold of members/board. Officer has **access to all books, accounts, vouchers, securities, stock, cash**, may take custody on serious irregularity, and may call board/general meetings. MSCS requires **≥15 days' notice**; state section numbers (inquiry/inspection) vary **[NV per state]**.
