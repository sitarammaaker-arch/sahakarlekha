# SahakarLekha Research Task 2 — Indian Cooperative Accounting: Domain Requirements

**Scope:** Domain knowledge only (no competitors, no software features, no product comparison). One page per cooperative type; tables preferred; uncertain items flagged **[NV]** = Needs Verification.
**Prepared:** 2026-07-08

**Cross-cutting note (applies to all types unless overridden):** Every registered cooperative is governed by its **State Cooperative Societies Act & Rules** (or the **Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act, 2002** if multi-state) plus its **registered bye-laws**. Common statutory appropriations before surplus distribution: **Statutory Reserve Fund — at least 25% of net profit** (percentage varies by state act; commonly 25%); **Co-operative Education Fund — ~1–5% of net profit** (state-dependent, **[NV]** for exact %); optional Dividend Equalisation, Building, Bad-Debt, Development funds per bye-laws. **Dividend on share capital is capped** by the state act/bye-laws (**[NV]** for exact ceiling per state). **Statutory audit is mandatory annually** by the Registrar / a Registrar-empanelled auditor or Chartered Accountant, with societies graded **A / B / C / D** (audit classification). Books must be preserved for the period the state rules prescribe (commonly ~8–10 years). These are stated once here and not repeated per type.

## 1. PACS — Primary Agricultural Credit Society

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Short/medium-term crop & agri loans (incl. KCC) to members; acceptance of member deposits; distribution of inputs (fertiliser, seed, pesticide); PDS/fair-price-shop retailing; sometimes procurement, storage/godown, and non-credit business (25+ activities under Model Bye-Laws). |
| **Typical sources of income** | Interest on loans/advances; interest on investments & DCCB balances; commission/margin on input & PDS sales; service/processing fees; dividend from DCCB shareholding; government subsidy/interest subvention pass-through. |
| **Major expenses** | Interest on member deposits & on DCCB/StCB borrowings; salaries & honoraria; cost of inputs sold; provision for overdue loans/NPAs; audit fee; office & administrative costs. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash book / day book; general & personal (member) ledgers; **Member/Share register**; Loan ledger & loan application register; Deposit registers (SB/FD/RD); Stock register (inputs/PDS); Investment register; Overdue/demand-collection-balance (DCB) register; Minutes books. Maintained per **NABARD Common Accounting System (CAS)** — accrual basis, standard GL, NPA/prudential norms. |
| **Key financial statements** | Receipts & Payments; Trading / Profit & Loss (with separate credit vs non-credit segments under CAS); Balance Sheet; DCB statement. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Annual audited accounts to Registrar & DCCB/StCB; NABARD/DCCB returns; NPA & DCB statements; CAS-MIS returns; GST returns on input/PDS trading where applicable; TDS returns. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Interest accrual on loans & deposits; NPA classification & provisioning; stock valuation; reconciliation with DCCB; surplus appropriation (reserve/education/dividend); balance confirmation from borrowers. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; NABARD/RBI prudential & NPA norms; CAS-MIS adoption; audit classification; KYC for deposits; GST/TDS on trading. |
| **Common audit observations** | High overdue loans / poor recovery; inadequate NPA provisioning; benami/ineligible loans; non-reconciliation with DCCB; input stock shortages; overdue interest wrongly taken to income; missing loan bonds/vouchers. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Thin margins & dependence on DCCB refinance; ageing, low-skill secretaries; manual/legacy record-keeping; recovery pressure; mixing of credit and non-credit funds; capital adequacy. |

## 2. Marketing Cooperative Society

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Aggregating, grading, pooling and selling members' agricultural produce; supply of inputs to members; procurement (incl. MSP operations as state/NAFED agent); storage/warehousing; sometimes processing. |
| **Typical sources of income** | Commission / margin on produce sold; **pool surplus** (net sale proceeds over pooled costs); trading margin on inputs; storage/warehouse charges; handling/procurement commission from federation/NAFED; interest on investments. |
| **Major expenses** | Payments to members for produce; transport, grading, packing, handling & storage; interest on cash-credit/working-capital loans; commission to agents; salaries; market fees/cess; wastage & shrinkage. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash/bank book; general & member ledgers; **Pool account registers** (per commodity/season); Purchase & Sales registers; Stock/godown register; Commission register; Advances-to-members register; Share/member register; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Pool-wise Trading account; Profit & Loss; Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; commodity-wise stock & pool-settlement statements. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts to Registrar; federation/NAFED settlement statements; MSP/procurement account to sponsoring agency; GST returns (taxable commodities); TDS; APMC/market-fee returns **[NV — state-specific]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Pool closure & settlement to members; stock valuation; reconciliation of consignment/agency accounts with federation; provision for dead stock/price loss; surplus appropriation. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; APMC/market regulations; agency terms with NAFED/state federation; GST on notified commodities; FSSAI where produce is processed/packed **[NV]**. |
| **Common audit observations** | Delayed/incomplete pool settlements; unreconciled agency/consignment accounts; stock shortages & shrinkage beyond norms; advances to members unrecovered; commission wrongly computed; price-loss provision inadequate. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Price volatility & carrying risk; working-capital dependence; delayed federation/agency payments; storage losses; competition from private traders; seasonality. |

## 3. Consumer Cooperative Society

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Retail (and sometimes wholesale) sale of consumer goods/groceries to members & public through society stores/fair-price shops; bulk purchasing; occasionally PDS distribution. |
| **Typical sources of income** | Gross margin on sales (sales − cost of goods); PDS/agency commission; rebate/discount from suppliers & wholesale federation; interest on investments; membership fees. |
| **Major expenses** | Cost of goods purchased; salaries; rent, electricity, store running costs; transport; shrinkage/pilferage & expiry losses; interest on working capital; audit fee. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash/bank book; general ledger; **Purchase & Sales registers**; **Stock/inventory register** (item-wise); supplier & creditor ledgers; member/share register; rebate register; counter/POS cash summaries; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Trading account (gross profit); Profit & Loss; Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; stock summary. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts to Registrar; GST returns (major cost/compliance area); TDS; PDS/essential-commodity returns where a fair-price shop **[NV — state]**; weights & measures/legal-metrology compliance **[NV]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Physical stock-taking & valuation (lower of cost/NRV); slow-moving/expired stock write-off; supplier & rebate reconciliation; gross-margin analysis; surplus appropriation incl. optional **patronage rebate** to members. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; GST; legal metrology/packaged-commodity rules; FSSAI for food items; PDS licensing where applicable. |
| **Common audit observations** | Stock shortages/pilferage; unrealistic gross-margin vs purchases; expired/dead stock not written off; cash-sales suppression; supplier rebates not accounted; credit sales to members unrecovered. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Thin retail margins; competition from private retail & e-commerce; inventory shrinkage; working-capital/credit-sale strain; billing accuracy at counter. |

## 4. Dairy Cooperative Society (Village-level DCS)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Twice-daily collection of members' milk; quality testing (Fat/SNF); onward sale to the District Milk Union; payment to producers; sale of cattle feed/veterinary inputs/AI services to members. |
| **Typical sources of income** | Commission/margin from the Milk Union on milk supplied; margin on cattle-feed & input sales; incentives/quality bonus from Union/Federation; interest on investments. |
| **Major expenses** | Payment to member-producers for milk (bulk of outflow, on Fat/SNF basis); society staff (tester, secretary, helper) honoraria; testing chemicals & equipment upkeep; electricity; cost of feed/inputs sold; transport (often borne by Union) **[NV]**. |
| **Core books & registers** | **Daily Milk Collection register** (member-wise, shift-wise: qty, Fat%, SNF, rate, amount); **Fat/SNF Test register**; Producer payment register / passbooks; Cash/day book; general & member ledgers; Cattle-feed & input stock register; Share/member register; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Milk purchase & payment statement; Trading / Profit & Loss; Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; periodic (often 10-day/fortnightly) producer-payment settlement. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts to Registrar; settlement/reconciliation statements with Milk Union; GST on cattle feed/inputs where taxable **[NV]**; TDS; milk-quality/FSSAI records **[NV]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Reconciliation of milk supplied vs Union credit; producer payment reconciliation; feed/input stock valuation; quality-bonus settlement; surplus appropriation; equipment depreciation. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; NDDB/Union operational norms; FSSAI/quality standards; weights-&-measures for weighing/testing; GST on inputs. |
| **Common audit observations** | Milk qty/value not reconciling with Union; Fat/SNF-to-payment rate errors; short/excess producer payments; feed-stock shortages; testing-equipment calibration lapses; incentive not passed to producers. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Very thin society margin (pass-through model); daily cash-flow for producer payment; quality/adulteration control; equipment reliability; producer loyalty vs private buyers; seasonality (flush/lean). |

## 5. Housing Cooperative Society (Co-op Housing Society / CHS)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Managing a residential building/complex on behalf of member-flat-owners: maintenance, common services, statutory funds; regulating share/flat transfers. (Maharashtra Model Bye-Laws used as reference; other states differ **[NV]**.) |
| **Typical sources of income** | Member maintenance/service charges; contributions to Repair & Sinking funds; **transfer premium** (Maharashtra: capped at ₹25,000); non-occupancy charges; interest on fund deposits; parking, rent of common areas, NOC/other charges. |
| **Major expenses** | Building repairs & maintenance; common electricity, water, housekeeping, security; property tax; managing-committee & audit expenses; lift/AMC; insurance; establishment costs. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash/bank book; general ledger; **Register of Members (Form I) & Register of Shares (Form J)**; **"I" & "J" registers** for members/shares; property/nomination register; Sinking-fund & Repair-fund ledgers; member-wise maintenance-dues register; receipt & payment vouchers; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Income & Expenditure account; Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; member-wise outstanding-dues statement; fund-wise (sinking/repair) statements. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts + audit rectification report to Registrar; annual return (Maharashtra **Form Y / M-20 filings** **[NV — exact form]**); TDS on contractor/professional payments; income-tax return (mutuality vs interest income); GST if turnover/commercial-use crosses threshold **[NV]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Sinking-fund (min 0.25%/annum of construction cost) & repair-fund (≈0.75%) accrual; member-dues reconciliation; provision for bad/doubtful dues; AGM within statutory time; surplus/deficit carry; conveyance/property records update. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act, Rules & Model Bye-Laws; timely AGM & audit; statutory fund maintenance; transfer-procedure limits; income-tax (principle of mutuality); RERA/conveyance where relevant **[NV]**. |
| **Common audit observations** | Large member arrears not provided for; sinking/repair fund short-collected or diverted; transfer premium in excess of ceiling; non-conduct/late AGM; unvouched expenses; investments not in permitted securities. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Member-dues recovery; underfunded major repairs; volunteer/committee turnover & record continuity; disputes on charges/transfers; ageing building capex. |

## 6. Labour Cooperative Society (Labour-Contract Cooperative)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Undertaking labour/works contracts (construction, loading/unloading, forestry, civic works) collectively; securing contracts from govt/PSU/private principals; deploying member-labour and disbursing wages/earnings. |
| **Typical sources of income** | Contract receipts / works-bills from principals; society margin/service charge retained from contract value; interest on deposits; sometimes hire of tools/equipment. |
| **Major expenses** | Wages/earnings to member-labourers (bulk of outflow); statutory labour costs (ESI/PF/bonus where applicable); tools, materials & site expenses; security deposit/EMD forgone; supervision & admin; interest on working capital. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash/day book; general & member ledgers; **Muster roll & Wage register**; Register of works/contracts; Measurement-book (MB) reference/bills-raised register; Deductions, Overtime, Advances & Fines registers; Share/member register; EMD/security-deposit register; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Contract-wise/works Profit & Loss (or Income & Expenditure); Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; work-in-progress / running-bill statement. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts to Registrar; **GST on works contract**; TDS (income-tax + labour); PF/ESI returns where coverage applies; labour-law registers under Contract Labour (R&A) Act / Code on Wages **[NV — applicability to member-labour cooperatives]**; principal's WCT/retention reconciliation. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Valuation of work-in-progress & running bills; retention-money & security-deposit reconciliation; wage-dues reconciliation; provision for contract losses/liquidated damages; surplus appropriation. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; GST works-contract; TDS/PF/ESI; wage-payment timelines (by 7th of month for the wage period); labour-law registers per contract terms. |
| **Common audit observations** | Wages/muster-roll not tallying with contract billing; retention/security-deposit not reconciled; unrecorded site expenses; contract-loss not provided; statutory dues (PF/ESI/GST/TDS) short-paid; earnings distribution vs member work-days mismatched. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Dependence on tender awards; delayed principal payments & retention lock-up; wage cash-flow; competition from private contractors; margin squeeze; irregular work volume. |

## 7. Industrial Cooperative Society (Artisan / Handloom / Producer-Industrial)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Organising member-artisans/weavers for production of goods (handloom/textiles, handicrafts, small manufactures); supply of raw material (yarn/inputs) & designs to members; collection of finished goods; sale/marketing (own outlets, apex federation, or govt emporia). |
| **Typical sources of income** | Sale of finished products; margin between sale price and (raw material + member wage/piece-rate + costs); govt schemes/rebate/subsidy (e.g., handloom rebate) **[NV — scheme-specific]**; interest on investments. |
| **Major expenses** | Raw material (yarn/inputs); **wages/piece-rate to member-producers** (per metre woven / units / design complexity); dyeing/processing; marketing & outlet costs; interest on working capital; salaries; wastage. |
| **Core books & registers** | Cash/day book; general & member ledgers; **Raw-material stock register**; **Production register** (issue of material → finished goods, member-wise); Finished-goods stock register; Wage/piece-rate register; Sales register; Share/member register; Minutes. |
| **Key financial statements** | Manufacturing / Trading account; Profit & Loss; Balance Sheet; Receipts & Payments; cost / production statements. |
| **Important statutory reports** | Audited accounts to Registrar; GST on products/inputs; TDS; scheme-utilisation/rebate-claim statements to Handloom/Industries dept **[NV]**; PF/ESI where wage-employees exist **[NV]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Physical stock-taking of raw material, WIP & finished goods; valuation; wastage-norm review; production-cost & wage reconciliation; subsidy/rebate claim reconciliation; surplus appropriation. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | State Coop Act & bye-laws; GST; scheme/subsidy conditions; quality/geographical-indication norms where applicable **[NV]**; labour statutes if employees engaged. |
| **Common audit observations** | Raw-material/finished-goods stock shortages; WIP not valued; wastage beyond norms; wage/piece-rate records incomplete; subsidy/rebate claims unreconciled or overstated; unsold/dead inventory not written down. |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Working-capital for raw material; market access & unsold-stock risk; low & irregular member earnings; competition from powerloom/private/imported goods; dependence on govt schemes/rebate; skill & design obsolescence. |

## 8. Multi-State Cooperative Society (MSCS)

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Primary business activities** | Any cooperative business operating across **more than one state** (credit/thrift, agri, dairy federation, marketing, housing, etc.), registered under the **Multi-State Co-operative Societies Act, 2002** with the **Central Registrar of Cooperative Societies**. Activity set = that of its underlying business type. |
| **Typical sources of income** | Depends on business (interest/commission/margin/service fees per the underlying type); interest on investments; membership/entrance fees. |
| **Major expenses** | Per underlying business (interest on deposits/borrowings, cost of goods/services, member payments); multi-branch/multi-state establishment; audit; compliance & governance costs. |
| **Core books & registers** | Books per business type PLUS MSCS-mandated records: **Register of Members**; share ledger; minutes of general body/board; register of investments; and books of account giving a true & fair view under the Act. |
| **Key financial statements** | Balance Sheet; Profit & Loss / Income & Expenditure; Receipts & Payments; consolidated (multi-branch/state) statements. |
| **Important statutory reports** | **Annual return to Central Registrar within 6 months of FY close** (by 30 September for a 31 March year-end) — incl. audited accounts, audit report, annual report, and declarations on AGM & elections; **audit by a Chartered Accountant**, report within 6 months of FY close; GST/TDS/income-tax per business; sector-specific (RBI for banking-type) **[NV]**. |
| **Common year-end activities** | Consolidation across states/branches; statutory audit by CA; surplus appropriation (reserve ≥25%, education fund, dividend within cap); AGM within statutory time; return filing to Central Registrar. |
| **Major compliance requirements** | MSCS Act 2002 & Rules; Central Registrar filings & elections; CA audit; sector regulator (e.g., RBI for credit/banking multi-state) **[NV]**; GST/TDS/income-tax; governance (board, AGM, member rights). |
| **Common audit observations** | Inter-state/inter-branch balances unreconciled; delayed return/audit filing to Central Registrar; governance lapses (AGM/elections overdue); deposit-mobilisation beyond permitted area/regulatory breach; related-party/diversion issues (a noted MSCS fraud risk area). |
| **Typical operational challenges** | Multi-jurisdiction compliance & coordination; weaker on-ground supervision across states; governance & member-communication at scale; regulatory-perimeter ambiguity (esp. credit MSCS); reputational risk from sector frauds. |
